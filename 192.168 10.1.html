Connecting to your router

The generic installation process of an ASUS or any other wireless router follow these steps:

  1. Disconnect the modem from the power supply.
  2. Connect an ethernet cable on the back of the modem - if that's the case..
  3. Connect the “WAN” cable on the back of the wireless router.
  4. Plug another ethernet cable to the “LAN” jack on the back of the router and connect it to the computer.
  5. Connect cable modem and router to an electrical outlet.
  6. Turn on the power button.

Your router is the manager of the office or home Wi-Fi network. Taking the new router directly from the box and connecting it into the power might give you a basic network - some routers come pre-programmed, for instant use, or the router might need configuring.

For either case, as the preset network is less than suitable, you are initially required to login to your router at 192.168.10.1. In order to log in, you will need your router username and passwords before you can start to obtain a new network setup.

In some cases, the installment was done for you by your internet service provider - ISP. Still, there may still come a time when you require to login to 192.168.10.1, to carry out updates or various other changes.

  1. In order to connect to your router, you need to activate a computer system with internet accessibility, such as your smartphone, tablet, personal computer, or laptop computer. With the smart tool in hand, attach it to your router. For a smartphone or tablet, you can utilize the Wi-Fi network to connect. Nonetheless, a computer system or laptop computer attached to the router with a wired ethernet link is the best option to ensure connection stability.
  2. As soon as you have attached your device to your router, open up your preferred internet browser. Your browser can be Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, Brave, or Safari. When you have your web browser open, you need to type 192.168.10.1 in the search bar.
  3. As soon as you have entered 192.168.10.1 (and not 192.168 10.1), you will need to obtain the required credentials to the login page for your router. Before you can make any modifications to your router, you will certainly have to visit the interface utilizing the login information connected with your router.

The first time you try to login into your router, the details will be the default ones associated with your router, which you can find on the back of your router. If there is no such information on the back of your router, you may be able to discover the login information in the guidebook of your router.

If you tried both options, you can search for the brand as well as the version of your router online. Note that some models of routers will not require any type of details for the initial login and you can just try to log in with no detail.

Changing the router default password

Passwords are the key to almost everything nowadays, and you probably have multiple passwords that you use throughout the day.

When it comes to your router password you shall be very careful. Imagine a hacker breaching your router and installing malicious software that will capture your most sensitive data (or money from online banking!). For sure you don’t want that. In this respect be wise and change your default password with a solid 12 to 16 characters password.

Complexity counts, even though some might find complex passwords hard to remember, are hard to guess even by brute force. General practice is to use capitals, special characters, and numbers in your password.

If you are afraid you are not going to remember your password you can always use a trusted password manager. Some suggestions are listed below:

  2. KeePass: https://keepass.info/
  3. Keeper: https://keepersecurity.com/
  4. Password Safe: https://pwsafe.org/

Changing the router default IP address

There are multiple reasons, one might want to change the default IP address of the router, and most of them are related to security. Anyway, before changing your router’s local IP address, make sure you take note of that new IP address.

After changing the IP address you won’t be able to use 192.168.10.1 address to access your router. Instead, you will have to use the new IP address that you just changed. In order to change the IP address you have to follow the following steps:

  1. Go to the setup or admin menu option
  2. Look for the network settings.
  3. Under the router settings, type in your desired IP address
  4. Save or apply the changes.

Changing the default name of the WiFi network

Because, most likely, you want to differentiate your Wi-Fi network from the other WiFi networks nearby, you change WiFi network name. In order to do so, make sure you choose a descriptive, friendly and not offensive network name to use. In order to do so you need to locate the WiFi setup area of your router menu:

  1. Go to the setup or admin menu option
  2. Look for the WiFi network settings.
  3. Click on the options and look for SSID
  4. Change the current SSID with your desired network name
  5. Click save or apply

Once you apply the changes, the router will restart and all the WiFi connected devices will lose connection with the router. In this respect, you will have to make sure you reconnect on the new WiFi.

Creating a guest WiFi network

New router models have included this feature to be able to run multiple WiFi networks with a single device. This is such a useful thing to do, especially for security reasons, when you have guests in your home or office that need to connect to a WiFi network to access the internet. Since you don’t trust those devices are not infected, and therefore to protect your main network from attacks, you better create this separate guest network.

If you decide to create such a guest network, you will still need to apply security measures in order to make it secure and not be vulnerable to hackers.

Maintaining the security of your main network and guest network shall be a priority when setting things up.

The option to create guest networks is generally available in the WiFi network section of your router and depends on the brand or manufacturer. With the new guest network, your IP address will be different from the 192.168.10.1 IP address.

What is the purpose of 192.168.10.1 IP Address?

An IP address - Internet Protocol address - is a sequence of numbers that identifies any device or computer on a network. Computers use IP addresses to communicate with each other both over the internet as well as on other networks.

192.168.10.1 is known as a private, local, or gateway IP address that is considered the head of your network. 192.168.10.1 is used to both access your router’s interface and to allow smart devices to connect to the router.

You can think of your router's IP address as a PO box for your mail. Any packages you receive through the post office go directly to your PO box, though instead of you going to collect, your router will then send the post to the computer device that requested it.

What is the router IP Address?

Your router has the role to connect two worlds and “route” data between your local network and the outside network - most of the time the internet. At each end, namely the network ports, your router will get assigned an IP address.
The external port that connects your router with the external world is called WAN - Wide Area Network - and it gets a Public IP address.

The internal network port is called LAN port - Local Area Network - and it gets a private IP address. Usually, this address is by default 192.168.10.1 IP address, and therefore only people connected to your network can see this IP address. Even though the router's private IP address is not unique to the device, in one network segment no 2 devices can use the same IP address. Please pay attention that there are no spaces included with the IP address, so 192.168 10.1 is not a correct IP.

For example in your LAN, your router has 192.168.10.1 IP address, your computer gets 192.168.10.10 IP address, your mobile phone (when connected to Wi-Fi) gets 192.168.10.11 IP address and your printer gets 192.168.10.100 IP address.


By using these IP addresses that uniquely identify the devices in a network, the router knows where and how to route data packets for each device and not mix them up.

You are entitled to ask who is deciding who is assigning those IP addresses? What are the rules when assigning them?

Those four sets of numbers separated by dots are also called IPv4 addresses and certain numbers are reserved to be used as private IP addresses. IANA or Internet Assigned Numbers Authority reserved certain ranges of IP’s to be used as private IP addresses. Those ranges reserved for private use are:

  1. From 10.0.0.0 to 10.255.255.255
  2. From 172.16.0.0 to 172.31.255.255
  3. From 192.168.0.0 to 192.168.255.255

Basically, we are talking about 18 million private IP's, but manufacturers still use a very small set when they decide the default IP address on their devices.

How to find your IP Address?

As explained, your private IP address, such as 192.168.10.1, can be assigned to your router by default by the manufacturer. If you are not sure of your default IP, you can often find it detailed on the base of your router, in the user manual that came with the router, or via the manufacturer’s website.
Some websites can show you the necessary details for your router's make and model. With a simple google search, you can locate such sites.
If that’s not possible, you can use your computer device to find your router’s IP address.

Finding IP address on a Microsoft Windows computer

  1. Click on the network icon, located at the bottom right of your screen.
  2. With the menu open, find the network you use for your internet and select properties.
  3. Scroll through this menu until you find Ipv4. This chain of numbers, similar to 192.168.10.1, is your gateway IP address.

Finding IP address on a Mac OS computer

  1. Select the Apple menu on the top left corner.
  2. Select the System Preferences.
  3. Select the network you use for internet access.
  4. Select the network you use for internet access.
